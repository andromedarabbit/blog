Someone on [quora](https://quora.com) asked:
> [What's the best way to document coding projects so non-technical people can make sense of them?](https://www.quora.com/Whats-the-best-way-to-document-coding-projects-so-non-technical-people-can-make-sense-of-them/answer/Roy-Tang)
<span class="ui_qtext_rendered_qtext"><p class="ui_qtext_para u-ltr u-text-align--start">It depends on what the purpose of the documentation for the non-technical people is</p><p class="ui_qtext_para u-ltr u-text-align--start">If you’re trying to convince them to try/buy your software, that’s marketing and copywriting, and you have to focus on feature sets and how it will be used by the end user</p><p class="ui_qtext_para u-ltr u-text-align--start">If it’s for users, to give instructions on how to use the software, typically developers will include some sort of user manual detailing the way to execute various tasks within the application</p><p class="ui_qtext_para u-ltr u-text-align--start">Regardless of the purpose, documentation written for nontechnical people will tend to focus on use cases and how the software satisfies requirements</p></span>
