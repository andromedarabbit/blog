After the debacle with last week&#8217;s manabase during the prerelease, I was more conservative with the colors. I resisted the urge to go for the additional removal offered by red and [Terminate][1]. [Lord of Extinction][2] and [Behemoth Sledge][3] were very tempting though, since they&#8217;re outright bombs.

My only two-drops were 2 [Grixis Grimblade][4]; based on some of my math analysis during the past week I would need at least 6-7 of each mana source for a 2-color card to have a 70% chance of being able to cast it on turns 2-3, so I went with 6 islands, 5 swamp and the borderpost. Between the [Jungle Shrine][5], the [Obelisk of Bant][6] and the [Wildfield Borderpost][7], I figured I had enough green sources to support the 2-card splash, especially since both cards don&#8217;t need to be played early. I put in plains for the remainder of the lands and felt quite satisfied with the mana.

**Round 1**

I open with [Grixis Grimblade][4] into [Grixis Grimblade][4]. I see [Spellbreaker Behemoth][8] and [Madrush Cyclops][9] but I have removal and the Grimblades go all the way. Second game went even faster.

2-0 games, 1-0 rounds

**Round 2**

Game one I take an early lead and lay down [Scourglass][10] for security. He shows me [Karthus, Tyrant of Jund][11] taking me to six. During my upkeep, I wipe with the [Scourglass][10] then lay down [Lord of Extinction][2] and [Master of Etherium][12] to go for the kill next turn. He untaps and shows me [Vengeful Rebirth][13] and we&#8217;re shuffling for game two.

Game two I have a 12/12 [Lord of Extinction][2] and he eventually runs out of chump blockers.

Game three, we&#8217;re trading on board, but I get a slight advantage with Shieldmages and he&#8217;s down to seven. I have [Cloudhearth Drake][14] and [Glassdust Hulk][15]. Eventually I go [Lord of Extinction][2] into [Sharuum the Hegemon][16] bringing back [Ethersworn Shieldmage][17], then declare combat. He thinks about hitting the Drake with [Intimidation Bolt][18] but realizes the Shieldmage protects the Drake so he tells me to swing and moves to block&#8230;

&#8230;but I point out the Glassdust Hulk is 5/6 and unblockable due to Sharuum. He can&#8217;t block either the Hulk nor the Drake so I take the win.

4-1 games, 2-0 rounds

**Round 3**

His deck is quick and aggressive, and we exchange close games one and two. However, in game three he curves out with [Goblin Deathraiders][19] into [Hissing Iguanar][20] into [Sewn-Eye Drake][21]. I put up some token resistance but in the end may hand was just too slow.

5-3 games, 2-1 rounds

**Round 4**

First game was [Glassdust Hulk][15] into [Behemoth Sledge][3] ftw!

Second game was [Grixis Grimblade][4] into [Grixis Grimblade][4] into [Tower Gargoyle][22] into [Behemoth Sledge][3] for another quick win.

7-3 games, 3-1 rounds

Didn&#8217;t get the 4-0, but still in terms of deck building this one was a lot better than what I did with the prerelease sealed pool. I&#8217;m thinking more clearly in terms of how to allocate the mana and how I&#8217;m going to play the early drops.

Next week is constructed (Regionals), but I don&#8217;t have any deck lined up yet and will most likely cook something up the day before. I&#8217;ll probably be playing something simple, aggro, red or all of the above. We&#8217;ll see next week.
